As the name suggests, the autocorrelation is the measurement of the pulse using the original pulse itself. Experimentally the original pulse is split in two equal parts and they are again made to recombine both specially and temporally using a variable time delay in one arm. A detector placed at the recombination point detects the interference between two pulses which in turn is converted to the autocorrelation spectrum. Depending on the measurement process this can be of two types- Intensity Autocorrelation and Field Autocorrelation.
